City of Miami

Along the southern coast of Florida lies the City of Miami, residing within Miami-Dade county. With it being the second-largest metropolitan area within the Southeast, and the ninth largest in the U.S., there is a myriad of attractions, historical sites, and plenty of growth opportunities for those looking to settle down.

Miami Quick Facts

With a population of 442,241, and a median household income of $60,989, this city is perfect for those looking for opportunities in finance, commerce, and international trade.

  • Has a gross domestic product of $344.9 billion as of 2017.
  • Incorporated in 1896.
  • Located in the Southeastern part of Florida, near the Florida Keys and Key West.
  • A booming urban city, especially for those working in healthcare research.
  • The southern side of the city inhabits the Coral Way, The Roads, and the Coconut Grove.
  • Western side of the city includes Little Havana, West Flagler, and Flagami.
  • From the Department of Agriculture, Miami lands under the 10b/11a plant hardiness zone index.


Geographical Insights

Miami can be the home of many diverse types of fauna and flora, especially being a prime coastal city. With the weather always permitting outdoor activities, the city is home to many different types of marinas, canals, and bays. Thankfully, this makes those who are into boating, canoeing, sailing, or even fishing, a great destination for these activities!

  • Home to numerous coral reefs, excellent for scuba diving and snorkeling.
  • With over 80 parks and gardens within the city, this makes it an excellent spot to see a good variety of the local flora on display.
  • There are over 3,000 different types of wildflowers in Florida.
  • Some of the most popular cultural destinations lie within the city, including Zoo Miami, Miami Seaquariam, and Jungle Island.
  • Bordering the western edges of the city is the Everglades, an infamous marshland that covers the majority of southern Florida.
