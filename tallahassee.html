Florida's Capital City

Tallahassee, the capital city of Florida, has a population of 201,731. It was incorporated in 1825, and became the county seat along with being the only incorporated municipality within Leon County. With it's population rising over the years, it has become one of the ten-most populated cities in Florida.

Tallahssee Quick Facts

Florida's Capital City is home to an astounding array of entertainment and performance arts, along with festivals and events being held annually. With our top employers being in the arts industry, those who are seeking artistic or entertainment opportunities will be thrilled to visit, or live within our state's capital. Here are some additional facts:

  • Tallahassee is classified as an urban city.
  • Our student population is growing each year, exceeding 70,000 in 2023, this classifies the city as a college town.
  • Home to the Florida State University, along with the Florida A&M University.
  • Since it is the capital, it is housing the Florida State Capitol, the Supreme Court of Florida, and the Governor's Mansion.
  • Fesitvals held in our city include the Downtown Getdown, Greek Food Festival, and the Tallahassee Wine and Food Festival.


Geographical Insights

With Tallahassee being relatively hilly, residing in the Red Hills Region, there is an array of different fauna and flora that you can only find here in the northern part of Florida.

  • Relatively moderate temperatures, in comparison to South Florida.
  • Fauna can range from the Northern Cardinal, Red-shouldered Hawk, Carolina Wren, and the Osprey.
  • While palm trees are present in this northern part of Florida, there is more of a variety of cold types like the Sabal palmetto.
  • Most flora and fauna found in Tallahassee are also present in South Carolina, and in Georgia.
