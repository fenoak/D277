The River City

In the state of Florida, Jacksonville, also known as The River City, is one of the most populated cities in Florida. Located in the Northeastern region of Florida, with a population of 985,843 this city has been deemed the 10th largest city in the U.S., and the largest city by it's area alone in the U.S. in 2020. Home to the Dames Point Bridge, the James Weldon Johnson Park, and the infamous Florida Theatre, Jacksonville has become just as sought after to visit as Miami.

Jacksonville Quick Facts

With Jacksonville one of the largest cities within the U.S., there is a plethora of attractions, business opportunities, and educational growth to be found here within The River City.

  • Has median income of $69,309.
  • Incorporated in the year of 1832.
  • Is classified as an urban city.
  • Initially introduced the cultivation of sugarcane, indigo, and cash crops from the British.
  • Has a total city area of 874.3 square miles. 86.66% of these miles are classified as land, and only 13.34% is classified as water.


Geographical Insights

Florida is the home of thousands of different types of flora and fauna, especially with its climate ranging from tropical to subtropical. Here are some quick facts about the land and life that inhabits Florida:

  • High temperature, though can become cooler in the panhandle of the state
  • Fauna can range from marine mammals, reptiles and birds such as the bald eage, peregrine falcon, and even the American flamingo
  • There are over 3,000 different types of wildflowers in Florida
  • Coconut palms are most prevalent throughout the state, no matter where you're located
  • Along the east coast, mangroves tend to be most popular
