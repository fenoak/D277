The Economy of Florida

Florida, the fourth-largest state in the U.S., has a population of 22,610,726 where the average income within the state is $67,917. Here are some quick facts about our economy:

  • Has a 1.647 trillion gross state product
  • Tourism, construction, international banking, healthcare research, and simulation training are some of the top contributers to our economic development
  • The largest part of Florida's economy is our tourism aspect, with us being home to the worlds most visited theme park: Magic Kingdom!
  • Home to the largest single-site employer in the U.S., Walt Disney World
  • Our agriculture is one of the largest parts of our economy, with us being the number one growers to foster oranges, mangoes, and fresh tomatoes


Geographical Insights

Florida is the home of thousands of different types of flora and fauna, especially with its climate ranging from tropical to subtropical. Here are some quick facts about the land and life that inhabits Florida:

  • High temperature, though can become cooler in the panhandle of the state
  • Fauna can range from marine mammals, reptiles and birds such as the bald eage, peregrine falcon, and even the American flamingo
  • There are over 3,000 different types of wildflowers in Florida
  • Coconut palms are most prevalent throughout the state, no matter where you're located
  • Along the east coast, mangroves tend to be most popular
